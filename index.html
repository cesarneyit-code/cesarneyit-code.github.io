Bio

I am a professor in the Department of Mathematics at Universidad de los Andes (Bogotá, Colombia).

I received a Ph.D. in Mathematics from Universidad Nacional de Córdoba (Argentina, 2009) and a B.Sc. in Mathematics from Universidad Nacional de Colombia (2005).

My research focuses on tensor categories and topological quantum field theories, with special interest in applications to quantum computation.

Research

Main directions of my current work.

  • Tensor and Fusion Categories: Structure and classification problems in fusion and braided fusion categories, including weakly integral and super-modular settings.
  • Topological Quantum Field Theory: Modular data, categorical symmetries, and constructions related to topological phases and topological quantum field theories.
  • Quantum Computation Connections: Applications of categorical and algebraic methods to quantum information, including braid representations and quantum error correction contexts.
